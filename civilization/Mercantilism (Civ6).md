# Mercantilism (Civ6)

Mercantilism is a [Renaissance%20Era%20%28Civ6%29](Renaissance Era) [Civics%20%28Civ6%29](civic) in "[Civilization%20VI](Civilization VI)". It can be hurried by earning a [Great%20Merchant%20%28Civ6%29](Great Merchant).
In the [Monopolies%20and%20Corporations%20%28Civ6%29](Monopolies and Corporations) [Game%20mode%20%28Civ6%29](game mode), it grants the ability to see the detailed amounts of all of the resources in the world. 
Strategy.
Mercantilism is the next step required to regulate an ever-spreading empire's commercial workings. Thanks to it, the outdated [Caravansaries%20%28Civ6%29](Caravansaries) [Policy%20Cards%20%28Civ6%29](Policy Card) is replaced by the modern [Triangular%20Trade%20%28Civ6%29](Triangular Trade), and further improvements of transport arrangements help the "wilder" parts of your empire to flourish.
The development of Mercantilism motivates hunters to trade rare skins overseas for other expensive goods. Thus, all [Camp%20%28Civ6%29](Camps) provide +1 and +1 . In "", it has the added benefit of elevating awareness of the commercial uses of tropical timber, which allows [Lumber%20Mill%20%28Civ6%29](Lumber Mills) to be built on [Rainforest%20%28Civ6%29](Rainforest) [Tile%20%28Civ6%29](tiles). If you're playing as the [M%C4%81ori%20%28Civ6%29](MƒÅori,) worry not that you won't get anything out of this arrangement, because your Forests will also gain 1 regardless of whether they're improved.
Mercantilism's long distance commerce also spurs the appearance of a new type of sea vessel: the [Privateer%20%28Civ6%29](Privateer). While the first of these were true pirate ships, crewed by desperate bandits preying upon innocent civilian merchants, states soon found out how useful such ships could be when attacking the "correct" targets, and "appropriated" them into their fleets.
