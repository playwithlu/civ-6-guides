# Solidarity (Civ6)/Civilopedia

During the Cold War, states put ideological pressure on their neighbors in a variety of ways. We can think of propaganda broadcasts over the border, leaflet drops, secret ideological cells, and, of course, the flood of hair metal and blue jeans into Eastern Europe. These shows of “soft power” were often organized and facilitated at the highest levels, and had a tremendous impact upon the eventual fall of the Berlin Wall and Soviet Union.
