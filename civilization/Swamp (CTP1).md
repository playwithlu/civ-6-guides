Great Library entry.
Malaria, typhoid fever, alligators, and snakes are only a few of the pleasant surprises that have made swamps historically inhospitable to civilization. Draining a swamp is a costly, time-consuming, and dangerous enterprise. Thousands of lives and over $390 million were used to create the Panama Canal.
