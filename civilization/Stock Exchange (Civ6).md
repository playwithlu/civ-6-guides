Strategy.
The Stock Exchange is a high-level building that boosts a [City%20%28Civ6%29](city's) output. In practice, it pales in comparison to most Tier 3 buildings, all of which require (and compete for) . It especially suffers when compared to the [Seaport%20%28Civ6%29](Seaport), which can also provide a large boost while simultaneously increasing , , and military capability without requiring any . The +11 for a powered Stock Exchange is often less than the available from running [Commercial%20Hub%20Investment%20%28Civ6%29](Commercial Hub Investment) [Project%20%28Civ6%29](projects) in large cities.
That being said, the Stock Exchange is cheaper to construct than most Tier 3 buildings, and can be boosted significantly by certain [Civilizations%20%28Civ6%29](civilizations) and in certain situations. In "", for instance, the [English%20%28Civ6%29](English) [List%20of%20civilization%20abilities%20in%20Civ6](civilization ability) gives an additional +4 to powered Stock Exchanges. For culturally-inclined civilizations, it can be used with the [Laissez-Faire%20%28Civ6%29](Laissez-Faire) [Policy%20card%20%28Civ6%29](policy card) to get important [Great%20Merchant%20%28Civ6%29](Great Merchants) like [Melitta%20Bentz%20%28Civ6%29](Melitta Bentz) and [Sarah%20Breedlove%20%28Civ6%29](Sarah Breedlove) that increase significantly. More importantly, with the [Ethiopia%20Pack%20%28Civ6%29](Ethiopia Pack), you can more than double the amount of produced by this building by having at least 6 s at multiple Trade [City-state%20%28Civ6%29](City-States), which makes this a very worthwhile investment and doesn't require [Suzerain%20%28Civ6%29](Suzerainty).
In the Monopolies and Corporations game mode, Stock Exchanges gain much more prominence: they are the only way for landlocked cities to store [Great%20Work%20%28Civ6%29%23Products](Products), which can provide very strong boosts to both and city yields.
