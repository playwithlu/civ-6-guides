Great Library entry.
Civilization grows on the backs of industry and labor. As long as there have been cities, those cities have been centers for the design and manufacturing of finished goods. By the third millennium BC, artisans had adapted the pottery wheel, one of the earliest production tools. In later ages, the pottery wheel would be succeeded by the loom, the factory, and finally the Nanite production facility.
