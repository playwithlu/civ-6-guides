# Pedro II (Civ5)

Dom Pedro II (2 December 1825 – 5 December 1891), nicknamed "the Magnanimous," was the second and last ruler of the Empire of Brazil, reigning for more than 58 years (April 1831 – November 1889). He leads the [Brazilian%20%28Civ5%29](Brazilians) in ".

Personality and Behavior.
Pedro will most commonly try for a [Cultural%20victory%20%28Civ5%29](cultural victory). If he pursues a different [Victory%20%28Civ5%29](victory) condition, he is likely to choose a [Diplomatic%20victory%20%28Civ5%29](diplomatic one) over a [Science%20victory%20%28Civ5%29](scientific) or [Domination%20victory%20%28Civ5%29](domination victory).
Pedro is exceptionally friendly and loyal, and will readily befriend anyone but the most warlike [Leaders%20%28Civ5%29](leaders). He is more willing to denounce than to wage war himself, but will maintain a defensive militia comprised of a variety of [Unit%20%28Civ5%29](units). He also has a habit of building a fairly large navy.
Fittingly, Pedro's highest priority is the of his people, followed by the development of his lands and . He will not claim a large [Territory%20%28Civ5%29](territory), but his [City%20%28Civ5%29](cities) will be highly populated and the land and water around them will be full of [List%20of%20improvements%20in%20Civ5](improvements). He will sometimes try to build [List%20of%20wonders%20in%20Civ5](wonders) that enhance his and output.
Pedro is friendly toward [City-state%20%28Civ5%29](city-states) in his sphere of influence and will often pledge to protect them. He will hardly ever attack or bully them.
Lines.
All of Pedro's Hate lines use a form of "you" ("vosmecê") that is considered to be formal and archaic, though not as formal as [Maria%20I%20%28Civ5%29](Maria's) lines.
Intro.
All Hail his most respected and magnanimous Emperor, Pedro II of Brazil. It was with great insight and unshakeable dignity that you ruled the Brazilian empire for over 50 years, ushering in a time of lasting peace and prosperity. Your reputation as a stern, but fair leader was never blemished by controversy, as your unwavering dedication to the people of Brazil provided hope and established the kingdom's position in South America. Your scholarly pursuits and staunch defense of freedom of speech led to the creation of several institutions of higher learning, strengthening Brazil's sense of national identity.
Revered Emperor Pedro, your people long for a return to the peaceful days when you walked among them, hearing their stories and reassuring them of a bright future. Will you once again lead your kingdom to prosperity? Can you build a civilization that stands the test of time?
