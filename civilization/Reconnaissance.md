# Reconnaissance

Reconnaissance (often called recon for short) is the act of using [unit](unit)s to explore the [map](map). Reconnaissance is an important part of strategy in the "[Civilization%20%28series%29](Civilization)" games and their spin-offs.
"[Civilization%20IV](Civilization IV)".
[Recon%20unit%20%28Civ4%29](Recon units) are those that specialize in reconnaissance. [Scout%20%28Civ4%29](Scouts), which are available after researching [Hunting%20%28Civ4%29](Hunting), are the first available recon units, and can upgrade to [Explorer%20%28Civ4%29](Explorers) after researching [Compass%20%28Civ4%29](Compass). Both units are unable to attack, but get only positive results from activating [Tribal%20Village%20%28Civ4%29](Tribal Villages). Scouts also receive a 100% strength bonus when fighting [Animal%20%28Civ4%29](animals).
Later in the game, [Spy%20%28Civ4%29](Spies) can be used to explore enemy territory and perform [Espionage%20%28Civ4%29](espionage) missions.
"[Civilization%20V](Civilization V)".
[Scout%20%28Civ5%29](Scouts), along with [Warrior%20%28Civ5%29](Warriors), are the first [Unit%20%28Civ5%29](units) available. Their ability to ignore [Terrain%20%28Civ5%29](terrain) makes them good at finding [Ancient%20ruins%20%28Civ5%29](Ancient Ruins), [Natural%20wonder%20%28Civ5%29](natural wonders), [City-state%20%28Civ5%29](city-states), and other [Civilizations%20%28Civ5%29](civilizations) early on. Scouts that find advanced weapons while exploring Ancient Ruins will upgrade to [Archer%20%28Civ5%29](Archers) and retain their ability to ignore terrain; however, they cannot be upgraded by normal means.
The [Shoshone%20%28Civ5%29](Shoshone) [Pathfinder%20%28Civ5%29](Pathfinder) replaces the Scout.
"[Civilization%20VI](Civilization VI)".
[Scout%20%28Civ6%29](Scouts), along with [Warrior%20%28Civ6%29](Warriors), are the first [Unit%20%28Civ6%29](units) available. Their ability to ignore [Terrain%20%28Civ6%29](terrain) and the special [Promotions%20%28Civ6%29](promotions) they earn make them good at finding [Tribal%20Village%20%28Civ6%29](Tribal Villages), [Natural%20Wonder%20%28Civ6%29](Natural Wonders), [City-state%20%28Civ6%29](city-states), and other [Civilizations%20%28Civ6%29](civilizations) early on. Or if you're lucky, finding a metor site left behind by a [Meteor%20Shower%20%28Civ6%29](Meteor Shower).
Scouts can upgrade to [Ranger%20%28Civ6%29](Rangers) after researching [Rifling%20%28Civ6%29](Rifling) (and [Skirmisher%20%28Civ6%29](Skirmishers) after researching [Machinery%20%28Civ6%29](Machinery) in '), giving them increased strength and a ranged attack. In ', Rangers can upgrade to [Spec%20Ops%20%28Civ6%29](Spec Ops) after researching [Plastics%20%28Civ6%29](Plastics), gaining the abilities to paradrop and directly attack support units in addition to extra strength and range.
The [Cree%20%28Civ6%29](Cree) [Okihtcitaw%20%28Civ6%29](Okihtcitaw) replaces the Scout, the [Incan%20%28Civ6%29](Incan) [Warak%27aq%20%28Civ6%29](Warak'aq) replaces the Skirmisher, and the [Scottish%20%28Civ6%29](Scottish) [Highlander%20%28Civ6%29](Highlander) replaces the Ranger.
