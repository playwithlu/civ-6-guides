Intro.
Your fledgling Australian colony seeks not to repeat the success found in New South Wales, but to exceed it. Can you gain fame in these desolate lands where so many others have failed? Will your fortune come from locating long-rumored goldfields, fielding massive herds of livestock, or preserving the land's natural beauty for future generations?
Gameplay.
In this scenario, you control one of four leaders trying to build a rich and powerful empire on the Australian continent. There is no [Combat%20%28Civ6%29](combat) - expansion, exploration, and [Resource%20%28Civ6%29](resource) collection are the keys to winning, and your victory progress is measured by your income (GPT). is locked, and the [Airstrip%20%28Civ6%29](Airstrip), [Mine%20%28Civ6%29](Mine), [Pasture%20%28Civ6%29](Pasture), [Hangar%20%28Civ6%29](Hangar), and [Airport%20%28Civ6%29](Airport) have been modified to provide .
All of the playable civilizations in this scenario have the [Outback%20Station%20%28Civ6%29](Outback Station) as a unique [Tile%20improvement%20%28Civ6%29](tile improvement). In addition to its effects from the base game, it provides +1 and gains adjacency bonuses to with [Flight%20%28Civ6%29](Flight) and with [Aerial%20Services%20%28Civ6%29](Aerial Services).
This scenario lasts for 60 turns. In the [Single%20player](single-player) version, you win by exceeding the GPT threshold (200 on Settler [Difficulty%20level%20%28Civ6%29](difficulty), +100 for each difficulty level thereafter) before time runs out; in the [multiplayer](multiplayer) version, you win by having the highest GPT when time runs out.
Since there is no combat, other perils have been invented for the scenario. These are manifested in the form of random outback encounters. Whenever [Unit%20%28Civ6%29](units) are left outside your [Borders%20%28Civ6%29](cultural borders), they have a chance of a random encounter, which may be either harmful or beneficial.
