Great Library entry.
Although the hot, dry plains seems like a poor place for human habitation, between 600 and 200 BC several cities south of the Sahara managed to exist and thrive. Jenne, Gao, and Kumbi grew in oasis and river regions which could support such large populations. Kumbi later became Kumbi Saleh, and capitol of the kingdom of Ghana.
